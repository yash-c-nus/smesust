How are companies scored?

Companies are assessed across 33 indicators grouped into 5 categories. The scorecard captures emissions tracking, energy and resource efficiency, environmental impact, workforce and social practices, and governance quality. Each category expands below to reveal its subsections.

Methodology Notes

📊

Data Window

Most indicators request data over a 3-year period, enabling trend analysis and direction-of-travel assessment rather than point-in-time snapshots.

🔀

Cross-Pillar Indicators

Some governance questions (Q4.6, Q4.7, Q4.9) are mapped to Carbon & Climate Performance for thematic coherence — targets belong with the emissions they address.

📝

Self-Reported Data

The scorecard is based on company-reported data. Transparency and completeness of disclosure are themselves indicators of maturity.

🏢

SME Focus

Indicators are calibrated for small and medium enterprises, recognising that SMEs face different constraints and operate at different scales than multinationals.
